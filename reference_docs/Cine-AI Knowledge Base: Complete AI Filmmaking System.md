## Overview

Welcome to the **Cine-AI Knowledge Base**, a comprehensive system designed to empower filmmakers with the knowledge, tools, and workflows needed to master AI-powered video production. This knowledge base represents the culmination of extensive research into the leading AI video generation models, audio synthesis platforms, and cinematic production techniques.

The Cine-AI system is designed to serve three primary functions:

1. **Expert Consultation:** Providing guidance on model selection, workflow design, and prompting techniques for every stage of the AI filmmaking process.
2. **Production Assistance:** Offering practical, step-by-step instructions for creating motion-ready stills, generating cinematic video, and designing professional soundscapes.
3. **Continuous Learning:** Maintaining an up-to-date knowledge base that evolves alongside the rapidly changing AI filmmaking landscape.

## Knowledge Base Structure

This knowledge base is organized into **12 core guides**, each addressing a critical component of the AI filmmaking workflow:

### 1. Motion-Ready Stills & Micro-Expressions Guide

**File:** `motion_ready_guide.md`

**Purpose:** This guide establishes the foundational principles of the Image-First Workflow, teaching filmmakers how to create still images that are optimized for animation and how to prompt for nuanced character performance.

**Key Topics:**
- The Visual Bible: Building a consistent aesthetic foundation
- Composition rules for motion-ready stills
- Animating with intent: Subject, camera, and environment motion
- Prompting for micro-expressions and emotional depth

**When to Use:** This guide should be consulted at the very beginning of any AI filmmaking project, before any video generation begins. It is essential for establishing visual consistency and character identity.

## How to Use This Knowledge Base

The Cine-AI Knowledge Base is designed to be used in a modular, task-oriented manner. Depending on the stage of your project and your specific needs, you can consult the relevant guide:

**Scenario 1: Starting a New Project**
1. Begin with the **Motion-Ready Stills & Micro-Expressions Guide** to establish your visual style and create your "Visual Bible."
2. Consult the **Shot Planning & Production Workflow Guide** to plan your shots and production workflow.
3. Use the **Video Generation Guide** (Model Selection Decision Tree) to select the best model for your project's needs.
4. Review the **Advanced Prompt Engineering Guide** to master prompting for your chosen model.
5. Consult the **Audio & Sound Design Guide** to plan your audio strategy.

**Scenario 2: Troubleshooting a Specific Issue**
- If your animated characters lack emotional depth, consult the **Micro-Expressions** section of the Motion-Ready guide.
- If you're struggling with character consistency across shots, refer to the **Character Consistency Across Shots Guide**.
- If you're struggling to achieve a specific camera movement, refer to the model-specific prompting techniques in the **Video Generation Guide**.
- If your generated video has artifacts, consult the **Quality Control & Troubleshooting Guide**.
- If your audio is not synchronized or lacks impact, review the **Audio & Sound Design Guide** for workflow optimization.

**Scenario 3: Learning About a New Model or Technique**
- Use the **Video Generation Guide** as a reference manual to understand the capabilities and prompting structure of any of the five covered models.
- Explore the **Continuous Learning Protocol** section of the Cine-AI Agent System document to understand how the knowledge base stays current.
