## Chapter 1: Traditional VFX Techniques

Understanding the fundamentals of traditional VFX is essential for any AI filmmaker. These techniques form the foundation upon which modern AI-powered workflows are built.

### 1.1 Chroma Keying (Green Screen)

Chroma keying is the process of shooting a subject against a solid-color background (typically green or blue) and then replacing that background with another image or video. While AI models can generate complex scenes from scratch, understanding chroma keying is crucial for compositing AI-generated characters into real-world footage or other AI-generated environments.

### 1.2 Rotoscoping

Rotoscoping is the process of manually creating a matte (mask) for an object in a video, frame by frame. Traditionally a painstaking process, AI has revolutionized rotoscoping, enabling the rapid extraction of subjects from their backgrounds. This is a cornerstone of the hybrid AI VFX workflow.

### 1.3 Matte Painting

Matte painting is the creation of a painted representation of a landscape, set, or distant location that allows filmmakers to create the illusion of an environment that is not present at the filming location. In the AI era, matte paintings are generated by image models, providing a fast and flexible way to create and extend digital environments.

### 1.4 Compositing

Compositing is the process of combining multiple visual elements from separate sources into a single image, creating the illusion that all those elements are parts of the same scene. This is the final step where all the different layers—live-action footage, AI-generated characters, matte paintings, and other VFX elements—are brought together.

## Chapter 2: The PBR Revolution

Physically Based Rendering (PBR) is a methodology for creating computer graphics that more accurately models the flow of light in the real world. The core of PBR is a set of texture maps, or "passes," that describe the physical properties of a surface.

### 2.1 Core PBR Passes

| Pass Type | Description | Use in VFX |
|---|---|---|
| **BaseColor (Albedo)** | The pure color of a surface without any lighting information. | The foundation for all lighting calculations. |
| **Normal Map** | An RGB image that simulates depth and surface detail. | Adds fine detail without increasing polygon count. |
| **Depth (Z-Depth)** | A grayscale image representing the distance of each pixel from the camera. | Used for depth-of-field effects and atmospheric perspective. |
| **Roughness** | A map that describes how rough or smooth a surface is. | Controls how light scatters, creating matte or glossy surfaces. |
| **Metallic** | A map that defines whether a surface is metallic or non-metallic. | Determines the reflection behavior of the surface. |
| **Specular** | A map that controls the intensity of specular highlights. | Defines the shininess and reflectivity of a surface. |
| **Alpha (Matte)** | A map that defines the transparency of an object. | Used to isolate the subject from its background. |

## Chapter 3: The Hybrid AI VFX Workflow

The most powerful approach to AI filmmaking is a hybrid workflow that combines the strengths of AI generation with the precision of traditional VFX techniques, supercharged by AI-powered tools like Beeble.

### 3.1 Workflow 1: AI Video as a Base Plate

1.  **Generate a base video** using an AI model like Higgsfield, Veo, or Kling.
2.  **Process the video with Beeble** to generate a full set of PBR passes.
3.  **Relight and composite** the scene to match the desired aesthetic.
4.  **Add additional VFX elements**, such as particle effects, text, or other graphics.

**Use Case:** When an AI-generated video has good composition and motion but needs a different lighting setup or needs to be integrated with other elements.

### 3.2 Workflow 2: AI-Powered Green Screen

1.  **Generate a character or subject** with an AI model, ideally on a neutral or simple background.
2.  **Use an AI rotoscoping tool** (like Beeble's) to create a clean matte, effectively creating a 
perfect green screen without the screen.
3.  **Composite the character** into a new background, which can be either live-action footage or another AI-generated scene.
4.  **Match the lighting** of the character to the new background using the PBR passes and relighting tools.

**Use Case:** Maintaining character consistency across multiple scenes with different backgrounds, or placing AI-generated characters into real-world environments.

### 3.3 Workflow 3: AI-Powered Set Extension

1.  **Generate a base scene** with an AI video model.
2.  **Identify areas for extension**, such as the sky, distant buildings, or landscapes.
3.  **Generate matte paintings** for these extensions using an AI image model like Nano Banana Pro or Higgsfield Soul.
4.  **Composite the matte paintings** into the video, using camera tracking data if necessary.
5.  **Match the lighting and color** of the extensions to the base video for a seamless integration.

**Use Case:** Expanding the scale and scope of AI-generated scenes to create vast, epic environments.

## Chapter 5: Best Practices for AI Video + VFX

### DO:

*   **Generate AI video at the highest possible resolution.** This will provide more detail for VFX work.
*   **Use tools like Beeble to create PBR passes from your AI-generated video.** This is the key to professional-level relighting and compositing.
*   **Plan your VFX shots before you start generating video.** This will help you create the right base elements.
*   **Use consistent lighting direction in your AI prompts.** This will make it easier to match the lighting of your VFX elements.
*   **Generate "clean plates" (simple backgrounds) when possible.** This will make it easier to rotoscope and composite your subjects.

### DON'T:

*   **Rely on AI to generate complex VFX in a single pass.** The best results come from a hybrid workflow.
*   **Skip the relighting step.** This is crucial for creating a seamless composite.
*   **Ignore lighting consistency.** Mismatched lighting is the biggest giveaway of a fake shot.
*   **Attempt manual rotoscoping.** AI tools are much faster and more accurate.
